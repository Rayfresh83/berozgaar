Is the paint blue?

Behold a room and canvas
I draw elegantly; a salad and water, blue
Strokes dense, perhaps warmer
But is the colour really blue?


Slow, the strokes depart, unbroken
I feel, though see things beyond
A rended-canvas beast drifts by
The town blooms in fire


The sky collapses thin
Limbs gangrenous rise from earth
The eyes are watching me claw
Dreaded, the ink might not be blue


The Pane sheds the fog
The paint, however, clogs
My feet are held in the dark
The room returns afresh


I see, for the shade of it
Has never been blue
Hue from the knife itself
Taken from the fallen
Nestling near my limb.
