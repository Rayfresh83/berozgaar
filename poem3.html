The bee and the flower

There was a bee, young once was thee,
A garden in bright and green,
Which held the hearts, flowers,
And lived the bees there free.
Taught were they by the mighty queen
What florets meant for thee.
Was all they did was pick some leaves,
And petals, heartfully.
Did learn too the younger bee,
By mimicking the flying frees.
An expedition onboard thus begun,
For the flower meant for thee.
Long did and tried, and so to find
The florets for the bee,
Off to the hive however back,
Resort was left to flee.
Though when the tidal tides did turn,
Did spot the wanted plea,
A floret, odd but blissfully,
Just waiting for the bee.
Time did fly, and so did thee,
Had formed a bond at ease.
The sorrow and despair that came,
Just all of it did flee.

Though it unlike is how usually,
Had not the bee seen—
Toxic, was what belonged,
The florets, chosen by thee.
Warn did the flying frees,
And the queen, say to the bee.
They said, “These florets need not be,
For you chosen specifically.”
Stubborn was the little bee,
Changing not the smallest feat.
Its love for what the florets was,
Unconditional, subliminally.
Then wait for the day did end,
The garden when was cleaned.
Drew out the fluffy roots of it,
Florets out of the greenery.
A terrible sight it was to see,
Sure did mourn this tiny bee.
If just it could potentially see
The harm it all meant to thee.
Is gone now the pain it brought,
And the bond of toxicity.
Still mourns for this little flower,
Our naïve, tiny, young—this bee.
